Banff National Park →

Surrounded by rugged peaks, the town of Banff offers superb shopping, dining, and nightlife — the perfect gateway to Canada's first and most celebrated national park.

Banff
Mt. Norquay Via Ferrata →

Scale the mountain with certified guides along a series of fixed iron ladders and suspension bridges — no climbing experience required, breathtaking views guaranteed.

Banff
Lake Minnewanka Cruise →

An hour-long interpretive boat tour sharing the park's rich history and local wildlife on Banff's largest lake, framed by dramatic mountain scenery on every side.

Banff
Banff Trail Riders→

Saddle up for a guided horseback ride through the mountain terrain of Banff National Park — an unforgettable way to experience the wilderness at a gentler pace.

Banff
The Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel→

The legendary "Castle in the Rockies" has welcomed guests since 1888 — a landmark hotel offering world-class golf, a full spa, multiple restaurants, and commanding mountain views.

Explore activities (5)
Canmore Cave Tours→

Descend into Rat's Nest Cave for a guided exploration featuring 7,000-year-old animal bones, ancient geological formations, and one of Alberta's best underground adventures.

Canmore
Carter-Ryan Gallery and Live Art Venue→

Visit the working studio of contemporary Cree artist Jason Carter for live demonstrations and an intimate look at Indigenous art and culture in the heart of Canmore.

Canmore
Yamnuska Wolfdog Sanctuary→

Join an experiential tour to meet and observe wolfdogs in their natural habitat — a one-of-a-kind encounter with one of Alberta's most unique wildlife sanctuaries.

Canmore
WinSport's Canada Olympic Park→

The 1988 Olympics legacy site now offers mountain biking, zip-lining, and downhill karting — year-round thrills on the slopes where Olympic history was made.

End the day in one of Calgary's most dynamic neighbourhoods, home to design-forward hotels, the National Music Centre, and a thriving cultural scene along the Bow River.

Heritage Park Historical Village→

Canada's largest living history museum features pioneer-era buildings, steam train rides, and rich Indigenous programming — bringing prairie life from 1860 to 1950 to life.

Calgary
Alberta Food Tours→

A self-guided tasting experience through the Kensington neighbourhood with GPS navigation leading you to the city's best local producers, artisan bakers, and hidden culinary gems.

Day 6

Southern Alberta

Today you'll move through wide open prairies and stunning badlands as you head to Southern Alberta.

Explore activities (4)
Hoodoos and Hoodoo Trail→

Wander through the surreal moonscape of the Badlands Valley, where wind and water have carved towering sandstone pillars — one of Alberta's most photogenic natural wonders.

Drumheller
Royal Tyrrell Museum of Palaeontology→

One of the world's premier palaeontology museums, home to more than 40 mounted dinosaur skeletons and hands-on exhibits that bring the Cretaceous era to life.

Drumheller
Dinosaur Provincial Park→

A UNESCO World Heritage Site preserving the richest dinosaur fossil bed on earth — over 40 species have been found within this surreal badlands landscape.

Brooks
Drive and overnight in Medicine Hat→

Journey east across the open Alberta prairies to Medicine Hat, a culturally vibrant city on the South Saskatchewan River renowned for its pottery, Indigenous heritage and sunshine.

Medicine Hat
1 hr 10 min drive 93 km (58 mi)
Day 7

Medicine Hat

Enjoy a culturally infused day filled with indoor and outdoor attractions, plus plenty of caffeine.

Explore activities (4)
Saamis Teepee→

Stand beneath the world's largest teepee — a 65-metre-tall steel structure built for the 1988 Winter Olympics and now a symbol of Southern Alberta's Indigenous heritage and pride.

Medicine Hat
Medalta Potteries National Historic Site→

Explore a 1912 brick-kiln complex that was once the largest pottery manufacturer in the British Empire, now a museum and active studio preserving Canada's industrial ceramic legacy.

Medicine Hat
Cypress Hills Interprovincial Park→

Canada's only interprovincial park straddles the Alberta-Saskatchewan border, offering lush, spring-fed highlands teeming with wildlife far above the surrounding prairies.

Cypress Hills
Hell's Basement Brewery→

A Medicine Hat craft brewery and taproom where locally brewed ales, IPAs and seasonal releases are poured alongside live music and a strong community spirit.

Medicine Hat
2 hr 35 min drive 213 km (132 mi)
Day 8

Writing-on-Stone Provincial Park and Lethbridge

Suss out a sacred site, a former whiskey trading post and a calming Japanese garden.

Explore activities (4)
Writing-on-Stone Provincial Park/Áísínai'pi→

A UNESCO World Heritage Site protecting the largest concentration of Indigenous rock art on the North American Great Plains — sacred to the Blackfoot people for thousands of years.

Writing-on-Stone
Fort Whoop-Up→

Alberta's most infamous whiskey trading post, now a National Historic Site in Lethbridge's Indian Battle Park where interpreters bring the wild frontier era vividly to life.

Lethbridge
Galt Museum & Archives→

Lethbridge's award-winning regional museum traces Southern Alberta's multicultural history from Indigenous Peoples to settlers, perched high above the dramatic Oldman River coulees.

Lethbridge
Nikka Yuko Japanese Garden→

A serene five-acre Japanese garden gifted by Lethbridge's Japanese-Canadian community — a living symbol of reconciliation featuring a teahouse, stone lanterns and immaculate koi ponds.

Lethbridge
1 hr drive 84 km (52 mi)
Day 9

Waterton Lakes National Park

Saddle up on a horse, dine at a grand hotel and spend plenty of time on the lakes.

Explore activities (5)
Waterton Lakes National Park→

Enter the UNESCO World Heritage Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park where the prairie grasslands meet the Rocky Mountains in one of the most dramatic transitions in North America.

Waterton
Alpine Stables→

Saddle up for a guided trail ride through alpine meadows and coniferous forests with panoramic views of Waterton Lakes — a classic way to experience this mountain wonderland.

Waterton
Prince of Wales Hotel→

Dine at this iconic 1927 National Historic Site with sweeping views of Upper Waterton Lake — a beloved castle-style hotel that has welcomed royalty, presidents and wanderers for nearly a century.

Waterton
Cameron Lake→

Hike to the shores of turquoise Cameron Lake — fed by glacial meltwater and ringed by peaks — one of Waterton's most breathtaking photography opportunities.

Waterton
Waterton Inter-Nation Shoreline Cruise→

Board a historic wooden boat to cruise the scenic international waterway between Canada and the US, passing towering peaks, wildflower meadows and abundant wildlife along the lake's edge.

Waterton
1 hr 25 min drive 118 km (73 mi)
Day 10

Southern Rockies

Venture into a colourful canyon and a former mine before bedding down in a charming country inn.

Explore activities (5)
Red Rock Canyon→

Walk the paved loop through this spectacular rust-red gorge, carved by Red Rock Creek through brilliantly coloured argillite, offering geological wonders and wildlife sightings at every turn.

Waterton
Drive to the Crowsnest Pass→

Travel through this iconic mountain pass connecting Alberta and British Columbia, a region with a fascinating history of coal mining, catastrophe and frontier resilience.

Crowsnest Pass
The Bellevue Underground Mine→

Descend into a real coal mine on a guided tour with a former miner, exploring the tunnels used by men who worked the Crowsnest Pass seam in the early 20th century.

Crowsnest Pass
Frank Slide Interpretive Centre→

Discover the story of North America's deadliest rockslide, where 82 million tonnes of limestone crashed down on the town of Frank in 1903, burying homes and lives in 100 seconds.

Crowsnest Pass
Country Encounters→

Rest at a charming B&B in the Crowsnest Pass, where rolling hills and mountain scenery provide a peaceful retreat after a day of exploring the area's remarkable heritage.

Crowsnest Pass
2 hr 32 min drive 229 km (142 mi)
Day 11

Southern Alberta to Calgary

On your drive back to Calgary, you'll stop at two significant historical sites, plus a distillery.

Explore activities (3)
Head-Smashed-In Buffalo Jump World Heritage Site→

A UNESCO World Heritage Site where Indigenous Peoples drove bison over a cliff for over 5,700 years — a masterwork of communal knowledge, technique and culture central to Plains life.

Fort Macleod
Bar U Ranch National Historic Site→

Experience one of Canada's greatest ranching enterprises where Parks Canada costumed interpreters bring the legendary cattle operations of the late 19th century back to life.

Longview
Eau Claire Distillery→

Tour and taste at one of Alberta's most celebrated small-batch craft distilleries in Turner Valley, producing award-winning whiskies, gins and liqueurs just 80 km south of Calgary.

Turner Valley

TERRITORIAL ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

In the spirit of respect, reciprocity and truth, Travel Alberta honours and acknowledges the traditional territories of the many First Nations, Métis and Inuit in Alberta. Alberta is situated within historical and present-day lands that Indigenous Peoples have cared for and lived on for generations. We honour the territories of Treaty 4, 6, 7, 8, 10 and their signatories. We also acknowledge the homelands of the Otipemisiwak Métis Government, including the eight Métis Settlements and the 22 Métis Districts. We acknowledge the many Indigenous Peoples living in urban areas throughout the province. We appreciate that modern borders do not align with traditional land use, and we honour the neighbouring Indigenous communities outside of Alberta who have travelled and inhabited this land throughout history.
