Explore activities (5)
Lake Louise Canoeing→

Paddle the iconic turquoise waters of Lake Louise with the Victoria Glacier rising above. Canoe rentals available at the Fairmont boathouse year-round.

Lake Louise
Fairview Lookout Trail→

A moderate 3.5 km hike above Lake Louise delivering stunning panoramic views of the glacial lake, the Plain of Six Glaciers, and the surrounding peaks.

Lake Louise
Columbia Icefield Adventure→

Ride an Ice Explorer onto the ancient Athabasca Glacier and walk on 10,000-year-old ice. One of the most accessible glaciers in North America along the Icefields Parkway.

Icefields Parkway
Sunwapta Falls→

A spectacular double waterfall plunging through a sheer limestone canyon. An easy 0.4 km walk from the highway makes this one of the Parkway's most visited natural stops.

Icefields Parkway
Maligne Canyon→

Jasper's deepest canyon features sheer limestone walls up to 50 m high, rushing waterfalls, and natural bridges over the torrent below. Multiple loop trails from 1–8 km.

Explore activities (5)
Maligne Lake Spirit Island Cruise→

A 90-minute scenic boat tour across the longest natural lake in the Canadian Rockies to Spirit Island — one of the most photographed landscapes on earth.

Jasper
Jasper SkyTram→

Ride North America's longest and highest aerial tramway to 2,277 m for sweeping 360-degree views across six mountain ranges and the Athabasca Valley below.

Jasper
Pyramid Lake Paddling→

Rent a kayak or canoe on this vivid glacial lake framed by the pyramid-shaped summit. A peaceful, scenic alternative to busier spots around Jasper townsite.

Jasper
Grassi Lakes Trail→

Hike to two emerald alpine lakes beneath dramatic cliffs etched with ancient Stoney Nakoda pictographs. A moderate 7.5 km round trip with rewarding mountain views.

Canmore
Canmore Nordic Centre→

Explore over 100 km of multi-use trails through the Bow Valley with mountain panoramas on every side. Open year-round for hiking, trail running, and mountain biking.

Explore activities (5)
Three Sisters Viewpoint→

Take in iconic views of Canmore's three towering dolomite peaks — Faith, Hope, and Charity — the defining symbol of the Bow Valley and a must-photograph landmark.

Canmore
Heritage Park Historical Village→

Canada's largest living history museum recreates prairie life from 1860 to 1950 with over 180 exhibits, heritage buildings, steam trains, and costumed interpreters.

Calgary
Studio Bell, National Music Centre→

An architecturally striking landmark celebrating Canadian music history with interactive exhibits, rare vintage instruments, a live performance space, and the Canadian Music Hall of Fame.

Calgary
Calgary Tower→

Step onto the glass floor and take in 360-degree views of the city skyline and Rocky Mountains from 190 m above street level on this iconic Calgary landmark.

Calgary
Stephen Avenue Walk→

Calgary's most beloved pedestrian street, lined with century-old sandstone heritage buildings, patio restaurants, boutiques, and live street entertainment in the downtown core.

Explore activities (5)
Glenbow Museum→

One of Western Canada's largest cultural institutions, with extensive Indigenous collections, western heritage galleries, and world-class visiting exhibitions from around the globe.

Calgary
Inglewood Bird Sanctuary→

A tranquil urban wildlife refuge along the Bow River with over 270 bird species recorded, 2.5 km of accessible trails, and stunning views of the city and distant Rockies.

Calgary
Calgary Farmers' Market→

Browse 80+ vendors offering fresh Alberta produce, artisan cheeses, baked goods, handmade crafts, and international street food under one roof — a true taste of the city.

Calgary
Fort Calgary→

Explore the original 1875 NWMP fort site and the story of Calgary's founding through immersive galleries, historic artifacts, and a scenic heritage park along the Bow River.

Calgary
Nose Hill Park→

One of Canada's largest urban parks, offering sweeping prairie and mountain views, Indigenous tipi rings, and abundant wildlife — deer, coyotes, and raptors — just minutes from downtown.

TERRITORIAL ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

In the spirit of respect, reciprocity and truth, Travel Alberta honours and acknowledges the traditional territories of the many First Nations, Métis and Inuit in Alberta. Alberta is situated within historical and present-day lands that Indigenous Peoples have cared for and lived on for generations. We honour the territories of Treaty 4, 6, 7, 8, 10 and their signatories. We also acknowledge the homelands of the Otipemisiwak Métis Government, including the eight Métis Settlements and the 22 Métis Districts. We acknowledge the many Indigenous Peoples living in urban areas throughout the province. We appreciate that modern borders do not align with traditional land use, and we honour the neighbouring Indigenous communities outside of Alberta who have travelled and inhabited this land throughout history.
